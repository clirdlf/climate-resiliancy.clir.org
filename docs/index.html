The 21st century has experienced a dramatic increase in extreme weather events. To address climate resiliency needs across libraries, museum, and cultural heritage institutions, CLIR and DLFâ€™s Climate Justice Working Group are hosting a series of free online climate resiliency workshops, informal climate circles, and conference meetings that will identify the key areas of action that librarians, archivists, and other cultural workers can take.

Through the generous funding of the IMLS Libraries Leadership Grant, the team will be recruiting a committee to establish a curriculum of talks and informational sessions allowing for the discussion of a wide range of topics related to climate change, systemic injustice, big data and its implications on the environment, disaster management and preparation, climate awareness, and community engagement.

The program will include a series of six online workshops on topics such as disaster planning, climate adaptation, and sustainable practices. We will also include less format climate circles to connect with other professionals and share ideas.

We are designing the program for librarians, archivists, and cultural heritage workers of all levels of experience. No prior knowledge of climate change or climate resilience is required.
